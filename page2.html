Sodium

Sodium’s symbol is Na. This comes from the German word for caustic soda: natronlauge. Sir Humphry Davy was given the credit of discovering Sodium in 1807 at the Royal Institution, London. He named it sodium because he used caustic soda as the source of the element.

Sodium’s atomic number is eleven, and the atomic mass is twenty-three. Sodium has eleven protons, eleven electrons, and twelve neutrons. Sodium is a metal. It is in period 3, and belongs to family 1a, alkali metals.
